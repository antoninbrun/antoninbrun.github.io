Hi, I'm Anto!


I am a first-year civil engineering PhD student at i-lab, part of the Viterbi School of Engineering at USC, working on Human-Building Interaction. I am advised by Burçin Becerik-Gerber.


I am passionate about occupant wellbeing, building energy efficiency, and occupant-centric design. Interdisciplinary by nature, my research draws from the fields of building science, human-computer interaction, and cognitive science. I aim to explore intelligent and responsive architectural spaces that support the needs of a building and its occupants, as well as interactive tools that allow occupants to connect with their spaces.




abrun@usc.edu
Curriculum Vitae

I have a MS in Civil and Environmental Engineering (focus in Sustainable Design and Construction) from Stanford University where I was a graduate researcher for Catherine Gorlé. I also have a B.Eng. in Bioresource Engineering from McGill University.
